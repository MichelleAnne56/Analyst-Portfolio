# Second Reserach Product 
My second research product is a look at China's value added trade from the OCED's Trade in Value (TiVA) database. I took a look at the foreign value added in China's exports as a percentage to see what countries have contributed to Chinese supply chains. I used the percentage of total value added rather than nominal figures to adjust for inflation and to identify broader geopolitical trends. The top 9 sources of value added in Chinese exports in 2022 were the United States, Chinese Taipei, South Korea, Japan, Australia, Russia, Saudi Arabia, Germany, and the category for the rest of the world. In 2022, 83 percent of the value in Chinese exports originated in China, which suggests that China's domestic industry remains quite strong. Also, no single country accounts for more than five percent of value added in Chinese exports, underscoring China's strategic effort to avoid over-reliance on any individual partner.
